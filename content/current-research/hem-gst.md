Hematopoietic stem and progenitor cells (HSPCs) are the precursors of all blood and immune cell types.
Their derivation from pluripotent stem cells (PSCs) in vitro has long been a central goal of regenerative
medicine, but true HSC-like cells capable of long-term multi-lineage reconstitution have remained elusive.

Our hemogenic gastruloid (HemGST) model recapitulates key aspects of human embryonic hematopoiesis in a
scalable, fully defined in vitro system. By directing PSCs through a gastruloid differentiation protocol,
we generate hemogenic endothelium that undergoes endothelial-to-hematopoietic transition (EHT) — producing
HSPCs with transcriptional profiles closely resembling those found in the human embryo.

We leverage single-cell RNA sequencing (scRNA-seq), CRISPRi functional genomics, and quantitative live
imaging to dissect the molecular logic governing HSPC specification, with the long-term goal of deriving
clinically relevant HSCs for transplantation.
